Ethiopian Holidays & Culture

Timket (Epiphany)

January 19 (Ter 11)

Celebration of the baptism of Jesus Christ. Features colorful processions of the Tabot and ritual blessings with holy water.

Orthodox Christian

Enkutatash (Ethiopian New Year)

September 11 (Meskerem 1)

Ethiopian New Year marking the end of the rainy season. Celebrated with new clothes, songs, and the exchange of gifts.

National

Meskel (Finding of the True Cross)

September 27 (Meskerem 17)

Commemorates the discovery of the True Cross by Empress Helena. Celebrated with the burning of the Damera bonfire.

Orthodox Christian

Eid al-Fitr

Variable (Islamic Calendar)

Marks the end of Ramadan fasting. Celebrated with prayers, family gatherings, and festive meals.

Islamic

Victory of Adwa Day

March 2 (Yekatit 23)

Commemorates Ethiopia's historic victory over Italian forces in 1896. Celebrated with parades and patriotic events.

National

Patriots' Victory Day

May 5

Celebrates the liberation from Italian occupation in 1941. Features military parades and memorial services.

National

Cultural Traditions

Coffee Ceremony

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is a significant social tradition that involves roasting, brewing, and sharing coffee with guests.

Traditional Attire

Clothing such as Habesha Kemis and Netela are worn during celebrations, reflecting Ethiopia's cultural diversity.

Dances

Traditional dances like Eskista showcase unique rhythmic shoulder movements and are performed during celebrations.

Food

Injera with Doro Wat and other delicacies like Kitfo are staples of Ethiopian cuisine, enjoyed during festivities.

Important Information

  • Time System: Ethiopia uses a unique time system, with the day starting at 6:00 AM (0:00 Ethiopian time)
  • Calendar System: The Ethiopian calendar is 7-8 years behind the Gregorian calendar and has 13 months
  • Working Hours: Most businesses observe holidays and may be closed during major celebrations
  • Fasting Periods: Orthodox Christians observe several fasting periods throughout the year
  • Regional Variations: Different regions may have additional local celebrations and traditions

Festival Etiquette

Dress Code

Modest dress is appreciated, especially during religious festivals. Traditional clothing is often worn during celebrations.

Photography

Always ask permission before photographing ceremonies or individuals. Some religious ceremonies may restrict photography.

Participation

Visitors are often welcome to observe and participate in festivals, but should follow local customs and guidance.
